How this shows up

I ran 100+ interviews before opening Figma. Every time.

For Protothon, 100+ interviews in 12 hours revealed a retention problem disguised as a discovery problem. For PocketPlans, 25–30 user sessions before touching code. The pattern is deliberate — I don't touch solutions until the problem is so clear the answer becomes obvious.

100+conversations before first wireframe, every time
How this shows up

Users told me to cut inputs. Data showed 26% dead clicks nobody mentioned.

On PocketPlans, users said simplify — I cut from 10 to 4 inputs. Then Clarity revealed a 26.67% dead click rate that nobody mentioned in a single session. What users say and what they do are different signals. I use both.

2×signals always used — qualitative and behavioral, never just one
How this shows up

I cut 6 features before launch. Every cut made it stronger.

Simplicity is the conclusion you reach after understanding what users need — not the starting point. Every feature I've removed has made the product stronger. The hardest product decision is often what not to build.

10→4inputs cut after user research revealed complexity was the enemy
How this shows up

Clarity was running before the first user arrived.

On PocketPlans, session recording and heatmaps were live from day one. That's how I caught a 26.67% dead click rate nobody mentioned in feedback. Data finds what conversations miss. I set up measurement before I need it.

97%scroll depth — data confirmed what users wouldn't say
How this shows up

The dead click rate became the entire v2 roadmap.

Clarity flagged users clicking elements that weren't interactive. No one complained — the data found it. That signal became the foundation of the v2 interaction redesign. I didn't guess what to build next. The behavior told me.

26.67%dead click rate → directly informed v2 roadmap priorities
How this shows up

Two AI products shipped. Neither started with "let's add AI."

PocketPlans uses API orchestration — not LLM generation — because hallucinated venues destroy trust. Structify uses LLMs because the output format is predictable enough to automate reliably. I choose AI where it solves the problem. I remove it where it creates one.

0→2live AI products, built where AI was the right tool — not the impressive one
